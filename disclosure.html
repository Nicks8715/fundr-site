Services

Nature and Scope of Financial Advice

fundr Limited can give you advice about financial products such as business, vehicle, and equipment finance and consumer finance. We specialise in sourcing finance for businesses and consumers by understanding their needs and then negotiating on their behalf with multiple financiers.

fundr is unable to offer legal or tax advice. We recommend you consult your solicitor or accountant for this type of advice.

fundr Limited is bound by and supports the duties set out in the Financial Markets Conduct Act 2013. These duties are:

Remuneration

How We Get Paid

For business, vehicle, equipment finance and consumer finance, fundr and the financial adviser receive commissions from the various finance providers we work with.

If you decide to take out a loan, the lender will pay a commission to fundr and your financial adviser. The amount of the commission is calculated through an agreed base rate between the lender and fundr. The adviser will then add a margin above the base rate to reflect the commission paid.

All fees and charges will be disclosed to you upfront upon approval from the finance provider.

Conflicts of Interest

Conflicts of Interest and Incentives

To ensure that our financial advisers prioritise the client's interest above their own, we follow an advice process that ensures our recommendations are made on the basis of the client's goals and circumstances. All our financial advisers undergo annual training about how to manage conflicts of interest. We undertake a compliance audit and a review of our compliance programme annually. We maintain registers of conflicts of interests, and the gifts and incentives we receive.

fundr and our financial advisers can receive commissions and brokerage from the finance providers on whose products we give financial advice. If you decide to take out the loan, the finance provider will pay a commission or brokerage to fundr at time of settlement.
